ABOUT ME

Welcome! I’m Karen Wang, a senior at the University of Michigan pursuing a BS in Environment with a minor in Data Science. I’m also an accelerated master’s student at UM School of Environment and Sustainability specializing in Geospatial Data Science. I’m interested in addressing environmental issues with data-driven solutions, combining GIS and remote sensing applications with clear, impactful data visualization and cartographic design.

I enjoy working with spatial data to study climate change, flooding, natural disasters, and conservation. Most recently, through NASA DEVELOP, I integrated satellite and ancillary data to create flood-risk and social-vulnerability models, informing conservation of unprotected wetlands in South Carolina. I was also a Research Assistant at the Allgeier Lab, where I analyzed and sorted invertebrate species from sand samples collected from artificial reefs. I have also done research with the Davis Rabosky Lab, where I studied the sensory systems of snakes in rainforest vs. desert habitats.

Outside of the lab, I also enjoy figure skating, drawing and painting, and birdwatching!
